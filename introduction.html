Introduction

Welcome to the website version of the Blockhead user manual! It will take a little while to get this caught up to the Google Docs version but
now that I have the chapter page template finished it will be a lot easier. Everything below is copied and pasted from the original introduction.

This is the completed first draft, which has been about a month in the making. Updates in the future will be much more gradual and occasional. Certainly let me know if
you find any errors or out of date sections! You can DM me (Hyfudiar) on Discord, or just @ me in the #documentation channel.

If the colors in screenshots look sort of inconsistently bright/warm/etc., it’s because f.lux evidently actually changes the colors of everything on the screen and doesn’t just apply a sort
of “filter” over it, meaning that I think it has been affecting my screenshots this whole time. Fun side effect of this is that you can tell roughly what time of day I worked on each section
on the screenshots! I might go through and redo them at some point, idk.

A note on frequencies and pitch - I regularly reference this webpage for translating between frequencies and pitch. I also perform most of my tests by sending audio from Blockhead into Ableton
Live. An odd thing about Ableton Live is that it considers C3 equal to ~261 Hz, which is generally considered C4 elsewhere. As such, there are some discrepancies between what is considered
C3 and C4 throughout this manual. This largely doesn’t affect much - if it doesn’t make sense as stated, just move it up or down an octave - but I wanted to directly address it here. I may
go back through and standardize it soon.
